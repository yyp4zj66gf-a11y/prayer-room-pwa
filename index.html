Why I built the Prayer Room

I built this Prayer Room app with several simple goals:

  • To encourage people to pray daily for a special list of people.
  • To encourage a daily prayer of praise.
  • To encourage people to ponder a Scripture verse every day.
  • To encourage reflection on one Salvation Army doctrine each day.
  • To separate praise prayer from petitions. In the petitions space, you can pray using just one or two words. Writing “church finances” says a great deal to God, who knows exactly what you mean because He reads the heart. The same is true of words like “exam,” “exercise,” “weight,” “surgery,” “work,” or “roof.”
  • Your daily prayer list is entirely your decision. Since no list can be endlessly long, I reserve mine for people who are uniquely special to me and support them in prayer consistently. Some days I linger longer on certain names because of circumstances I am aware of.

This way of praying has helped me for more than thirty years. If it helps you pray with steadiness and peace, you are welcome to use it — and to share it with a friend.

Privacy

PrayerSpace does not require an account. Your writing (names, prayers, and petitions) is saved locally in your own browser on your own device.
